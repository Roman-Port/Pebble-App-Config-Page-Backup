FAQ / Support

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I log in with my access code?

Just follow the step-by-step guide below to enter your access code and unlock your clock face:

Step 1: Open your Fitbit app and tap on your watch on the top left.

Step 2: Tap on the clock faces entry.

Step 3: On your currently installed clock face entry tap the 3 dots icon on the top right.

Step 4: Tap on "Settings".

Step 5: Tap on "Enter your access code".

Step 5: Enter the access code you have been given by email and unlock the clock face.

Important, please read:

Unfortunately every time you change your clock, you must log in again through that clock's settings page using your access code.

This is because when you change clocks on Fitbit, your settings and login will be wiped in doing so, which sadly is a "feature" of Fitbit's platform. We as developers have no choice in the matter. Every time you switch clocks, the clock's storage is completely wiped. We unfortunately have no way of knowing when you come back to our clock what your previous settings were or what your login info was.

If you'd like to help encourage Fitbit to change this behaviour, please vote on this community thread.

We can only hope that Fitbit considers changing this behaviour for future updates.

How/where can I access my clock face's settings?

Just follow the step-by-step guide below to get to the settingspage of your clock face:

Step 1: Open your Fitbit app and tap on your watch on the top left.

Step 2: Tap on the clock faces entry.

Step 3: On your currently installed clock face entry tap the 3 dots icon on the top right.

Why is there a splashscreen visible for a few seconds every time I return to the clock face?

The splash screen isn't just for cosmetics, it actually has a use, albeit a small one.

The reason we add the splash is because Fitbit's load times are quite slow (although they lately have been improving with recent firmware), so we take advantage of the gap in time between loading the bulk of our clock and the black screen you see to show the time. This way, for users quickly wanting to see the time out of an app, they get to see it a second quicker than they would without it. If we remove the splash screen, you will just see black for an extra second or two every time the face is loaded.

Seems petty, but if you run the arithmetic, it actually saves a lot of people a lot of time in the long run.

TypeError: Failed to fetch

It seems as if you are on a network which blocks certain connections. Please use another network and try again.

Settings stuck on initial sync

If our clock face isn't syncing after install, this is usually due to Fitbit's poor connection issues. Please try rebooting your phone/Fitbit and it should work properly.

I get a clock error message on my watch

"Clock Error" means that your Fitbit ran into an internal error - this is not our bug.

Simply try reinstalling the clock face again. If this continues to happen, something may be wrong with your Fitbit and we'd recommend reaching out to Fitbit support.

I purchased the lifetime bundle how do I get other clock faces?

Just browse this page and tap on the clock face you like or search for "Lignite" in the fitbit app's app gallery to see all of our clock faces.

Where can I change the clock to 12h or 24h mode?

You can change your time mode preference on your Fitbit account at Click here to go to your Fitbit account. Once changed, please resync your Fitbit and the new time format should apply. If not, simply reboot your watch.

Pebble Centre

Our Pebble legacy watchfaces

As many of you know, Pebble was sold to Fitbit in 2016 and shut down their services for all remaining Pebblers in 2018.

We have decided to end all of our Pebble services early in 2019.

All 25 of our Pebble watchfaces have been rebuilt to work without the need for our servers.

That means you can install any of our watchfaces without needing to log in through settings and without needing to be connected to the internet to use settings.
